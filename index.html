Scratchpad

Scratchpad is a PWA to store notes.

You can either store them on device or sign in to save them online.

If you store them on device, they will be stored in browser’s local storage and no data will be stored in the cloud. However, keep in mind that flushing your browser’s site preferences will remove your notes.

If you choose to sign in, you will save your notes in the cloud and be able to share them between multiple devices. Data are stored on a free Firebase storage (Google).

Be aware that notes are stored by account type without any link between them. That means that If you connect via Google, save a note and then connect with Twitter, note won’t be the same. You’ll have one version with Google and another version with Twitter.

That application is free and open source. It’s not a commercial product, only a « scratch your own itch » app. Code is available on github.

If you’re concerned about privacy and security, I encourage you to store data on device. Also, you can host it yourself by cloning the project on a web server and creating your own Firebase account.

More about my projects and contact informations on paulgreg.me. According to GDPR, you can contact me if you want to get or remove your data (only if you had sign in of course, I won’t have any data if they’re stored on your device).
